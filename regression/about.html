Correlation and Linear Regression

When two variables are related, it is possible to predict a person's score on one variable from their score on the second variable with better than chance accuracy. This section describes how these predictions are made and what can be learned about the relationship between the variables by developing a prediction equation. It will be assumed that the relationship between the two variables is linear.

Although there are methods for making predictions when the relationship is nonlinear, these methods are beyond the scope of this text.
